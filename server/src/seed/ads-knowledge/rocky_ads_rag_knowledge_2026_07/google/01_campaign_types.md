# Google Ads — Campaign Type Selection
Platform: google_ads
Knowledge version: 2026-07
Source: https://support.google.com/google-ads/answer/2567043?hl=en

Google Ads campaign type should be chosen from the advertiser's actual business goal, conversion goal, creative/inventory needs, and available measurement.

## Search
Use Search when the business needs to capture existing intent from people actively searching for products or services. It is especially suitable for sales, leads and qualified website traffic. Search should not be chosen merely because the advertiser wants "more reach"; it is primarily an intent-capture channel.

## Performance Max
Performance Max is goal-based and can access Google Ads inventory across channels from one campaign. It uses Smart Bidding and Google AI across bidding, budget optimization, audiences, creatives and attribution. It should be grounded in meaningful conversion goals. Google exposes Performance Max under objectives such as Sales, Leads and Local store visits/promotions.

## Video
Video campaigns use YouTube and other eligible inventory. Choose awareness/view-oriented configurations for reach or consideration; conversion-oriented configurations should have appropriate conversion measurement.

## App
Use App campaigns when the primary business outcome concerns app installs, app engagement or in-app actions.

## Demand Gen
Demand Gen is designed for demand creation and action across visual/discovery surfaces. See the dedicated Demand Gen knowledge file.

## Rocky rule
Never select a campaign type solely from the business industry. First infer: objective → conversion action → customer intent → available assets/data → campaign type.
